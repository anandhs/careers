## What this job is

Commercial air pilots operate aircraft for passenger and cargo transportation. They must ensure safety, comply with regulations, and manage navigational equipment while in flight.

## Day in the life

A typical day for a commercial pilot starts with a pre-flight briefing where they review flight plans, weather conditions, and aircraft status. After a thorough safety inspection, they communicate with air traffic control before takeoff. During the flight, pilots work closely with co-pilots and cabin crew, managing communication and navigation while ensuring passenger comfort and safety.

## Earning potential

As of 2026, commercial pilots earn between $85,000 and $180,000 annually, depending on experience, type of aircraft operated, and the airline.

## How to get there

To become a commercial air pilot, one needs to obtain a private pilot's license, followed by a commercial pilot's license, often requiring a degree in aviation or related fields and several hundred flying hours. Additional certifications, such as an instrument rating, are also beneficial.

## Skills that help

Key skills for pilots include strong communication, problem-solving abilities, decision-making under pressure, teamwork, and technical proficiency in operating aircraft systems.

## Leading companies

Major commercial airlines like American Airlines, Delta Air Lines, and Southwest Airlines are leading employers in the aviation industry, offering extensive training programs and career advancement opportunities.

## Risks and tradeoffs

The career can involve irregular hours, long trips, and time away from home, which may be challenging for work-life balance. Additionally, pilots face safety risks and the stress of managing emergencies.

## Next 10 year outlook

The aviation sector is projected to see steady growth over the next decade, driven by global travel demand. However, pilots should be prepared for shifts in regulations and technology advancements, such as increased automation.

## What to know before choosing it

Before pursuing a career as a commercial pilot, consider the high training costs, the competitive nature of the industry, and the physical and mental demands involved in flying large aircraft.
