## What this job is

A Director of Engineering oversees engineering teams, setting project goals and ensuring that technical solutions align with business objectives. This role involves strategic decision-making, managing budgets, and mentoring engineering staff.

## Day in the life

A typical day may include meetings with stakeholders to discuss project statuses, reviewing engineering workflows, providing guidance to team leads, and ensuring that the engineering practices align with industry standards. You'll also spend time on planning new projects and resource allocation.

## Earning potential

In Charlotte, Directors of Engineering can expect to earn between $120,000 and $200,000 annually, depending on experience and the size of the organization.

## How to get there

Most Directors of Engineering have a background in engineering, typically holding a Bachelor's degree in a relevant field. Progressing to this role often requires several years of experience in engineering management or technical leadership, along with demonstrated expertise in project management.

## Skills that help

Key skills include strong leadership, effective communication, project management, technical acumen, and the ability to foster team collaboration. Knowledge of software development processes and agile methodologies is also beneficial.

## Leading companies

Leading companies in Charlotte often include technology firms, financial services companies, and healthcare technology organizations, where you can find opportunities for this role.

## Risks and tradeoffs

The role can be highly rewarding but comes with stress related to deadlines and team performance. A Director of Engineering often faces pressure to innovate and maintain productivity, which requires managing multiple projects simultaneously.

## Next 10 year outlook

The demand for Directors of Engineering is expected to grow as companies continue to invest in technology and digital transformation. However, competition will increase as more professionals seek leadership roles in technology.

## What to know before choosing it

Consider the significance of balancing technical proficiency with people management skills. This role can also require long hours and may be less hands-on than engineering roles, focusing more on strategy and team dynamics.
