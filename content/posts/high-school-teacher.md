## What this job is

A high school teacher is responsible for educating students in grades 9-12. They create lesson plans, assess student performance, and develop a positive learning environment. Subjects can vary from core academics to specialized topics like arts and vocational training.

## Day in the life

Typically, a high school teacher starts their day preparing lessons and materials. They teach multiple classes throughout the day, grade assignments, and meet with students for additional support. Teachers also attend faculty meetings and professional development workshops, and spend time communicating with parents about student progress.

## Earning potential

The earning potential for a high school teacher can range from $40,000 to $70,000 annually, depending on factors such as location, years of experience, and educational background. Teachers in urban areas or those with advanced degrees often earn higher salaries.

## How to get there

To become a high school teacher, a bachelor's degree in education or a specific subject area is typically required. Many states also require teachers to obtain a teaching license, which usually involves passing exams and completing a student teaching internship.

## Skills that help

Skills that are essential for high school teachers include effective communication, patience, organizational abilities, and a passion for teaching. Additionally, being adaptable and proficient in technology is increasingly important in today’s classrooms.

## Leading companies

Major educational institutions and organizations providing teaching opportunities include University of Phoenix, Teach For America, and K12 Inc., among others. These organizations often support teacher training and professional development.

## Risks and tradeoffs

Teaching can be emotionally taxing, as it involves managing diverse student needs and sometimes challenging classroom behavior. Additionally, teachers may work long hours, including evenings and weekends, for grading and lesson preparation, which may impact work-life balance.

## Next 10 year outlook

The demand for high school teachers is expected to continue growing, especially in areas such as STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) and special education. However, budget cuts in education may impact job availability in some regions.

## What to know before choosing it

Before choosing a career as a high school teacher, consider your passion for working with adolescents and your willingness to engage in continuous learning and professional development. Understand that teaching involves not only imparting knowledge but also addressing emotional and social barriers to learning.
